## Key Findings

1. We propose HILBERT (HIerarchical Long-sequence Balanced Embedding with Reciprocal contrastive Training), a cross-attentive multimodal framework for learning document-level audio-text representations from long, segmented sequences in low-resource data settings
2. HILBERT leverages frozen pre-trained speech and language encoders to extract segment-level features, which are aggregated via cross-modal attention and self-attentive pooling to form modality-specific document representations and a joint cross-attentive embedding
3. To align modalities while preserving modality-specific structure under severe audio-text dimensional imbalance, we introduce a reciprocal dual contrastive objective that simultaneously aligns audio-to-joint and text-to-joint representations, rather than directly contrasting audio and text alone
